# CareVault User Manual

## 1. Introduction

Welcome to CareVault, a comprehensive medical information management system designed for chronic disease patients and their caregivers. This manual will guide you through setting up your account, managing patient information, tracking medications, scheduling appointments, and storing important documents.

CareVault provides a secure, centralized platform to manage healthcare information, making it easier for caregivers to provide quality care and for patients to maintain oversight of their health information.

## 2. Getting Started

### 2.1 Account Registration

#### 2.1.1 Creating an Account
1. Visit the CareVault website
2. Click on the "Register" or "Sign Up" button on the login page
3. Enter your email address and create a secure password
4. Verify your email address by clicking the link sent to your inbox
5. Complete your profile with your full name and phone number

#### 2.1.2 Account Types
CareVault supports two types of accounts:
- **Caregiver Account**: Full access to add and manage care recipients, medications, appointments, and documents
- **Patient Account**: Read-only access to personal medical information

### 2.2 Logging In
1. Navigate to the CareVault login page
2. Enter your registered email address and password
3. Click "Sign In"
4. If you have trouble logging in, use the "Forgot Password" link to reset your password

### 2.3 Dashboard Overview
After logging in, you'll be directed to your personalized dashboard:
- **Top Navigation**: Contains your profile menu, logout button, and quick access to key features
- **Patient Selection**: If you manage multiple care recipients, use the dropdown to switch between them
- **Statistics Cards**: Shows quick stats like active medications, upcoming appointments, etc.
- **Main Content Area**: Displays detailed information for the selected care recipient

## 3. Managing Care Recipients

### 3.1 Adding a Care Recipient
As a caregiver, you can manage multiple care recipients:

1. From your dashboard, click the "Add Patient" button
2. Fill in the required information:
   - First name and last name
   - Date of birth
   - Diagnosis (optional)
   - Allergies (optional)
   - Emergency contact information (name, phone, relationship)
3. Click "Save" to add the care recipient

### 3.2 Editing Care Recipient Information
1. Select the care recipient you want to edit from the dashboard
2. Click the edit (pencil) icon next to their profile
3. Update the information as needed
4. Click "Save" to apply changes

### 3.3 Deactivating a Care Recipient
1. Select the care recipient you want to deactivate
2. Click the trash icon in their profile header
3. Confirm the deletion when prompted (this will deactivate rather than permanently delete)

## 4. Medication Management

### 4.1 Adding Medications
1. From the dashboard, find the "Medications" section
2. Click the "Add" button
3. Enter medication details:
   - Medication name
   - Dosage and frequency information
   - Prescribing doctor (optional)
   - Start date
4. Click "Save" to add the medication

### 4.2 Managing Medications
- View all active medications in the medications list
- Edit medication information by clicking the edit icon
- Deactivate medications by removing them from the active list
- Filter medications using the search bar

### 4.3 Medication History
- View medication history sorted by date
- Track changes to medication regimens
- Monitor medication compliance

## 5. Appointment Management

### 5.1 Scheduling Appointments
1. Navigate to the "Appointments" section or calendar view
2. Click "Add Appointment"
3. Fill in appointment details:
   - Appointment title
   - Date and time
   - Location
   - Doctor's name (optional)
   - Description/notes (optional)
4. Set reminder preferences if applicable
5. Click "Save" to schedule the appointment

### 5.2 Managing Appointments
- View upcoming appointments in chronological order
- Mark appointments as completed when attended
- Reschedule appointments by editing the date/time
- Cancel appointments that are no longer needed

### 5.3 Calendar View
- Access the calendar view for a comprehensive schedule
- See all appointments in monthly, weekly, or daily views
- Quickly add appointments from the calendar interface

## 6. Document Management

### 6.1 Uploading Documents
1. Go to the "Documents" section for your selected care recipient
2. Click "Upload Document" or "Add Document"
3. Fill in document details:
   - Document name
   - Category (medical, legal, financial, identification)
   - Date (optional)
4. Select the file to upload
5. Click "Upload" to save the document

### 6.2 Document Categories
Organize documents into categories:
- **Medical**: Test results, medical records, insurance cards
- **Legal**: Wills, power of attorney, advance directives
- **Financial**: Insurance information, financial records
- **Identification**: Driver's license, passport, Medicare card

### 6.3 Managing Documents
- Browse documents by category
- Download documents when needed
- Delete documents you no longer need
- Share documents securely with healthcare providers

## 7. Emergency Information

### 7.1 Emergency Summary
The emergency summary provides critical information for healthcare providers:

1. Click "Emergency Summary" from the dashboard
2. Review the automatically generated summary including:
   - Patient demographics
   - Current medications
   - Allergies
   - Emergency contacts
   - Recent conditions
3. Use the "Export" button to download as PDF for sharing

### 7.2 Emergency Contacts
- Maintain up-to-date emergency contact information
- Designate primary and secondary contacts
- Include relationship, phone number, and email
- Update contacts regularly to ensure accuracy

## 8. Care Logs

### 8.1 Adding Care Logs
Track daily care activities and observations:

1. Navigate to the "Care Logs" section
2. Click "Add Care Log"
3. Enter information about:
   - Type of observation or activity
   - Details of the entry
   - Date of occurrence
4. Save the care log entry

### 8.2 Reviewing Care History
- View care logs chronologically
- Track patterns and changes over time
- Share relevant information with healthcare providers

## 9. User Profile and Settings

### 9.1 Updating Profile Information
1. Click on your profile icon in the top navigation
2. Select "Profile" or "Account Settings"
3. Update your information as needed
4. Save changes to apply updates

### 9.2 Managing Account Security
- Change your password regularly
- Keep your email address updated
- Review connected accounts if using social login

## 10. Role-Specific Features

### 10.1 Caregiver Features
As a caregiver, you have full management capabilities:
- Add and manage multiple care recipients
- Update all medical information
- Schedule appointments
- Upload and manage documents
- Generate emergency summaries
- Add care logs and observations

### 10.2 Patient Features
As a patient, you have read-only access:
- View your own medical information
- Access your emergency summary
- View your appointments and medications
- Contact your caregiver through appropriate channels

## 11. Search and Filtering

### 11.1 Searching Information
- Use the search bars available in medications, appointments, and documents sections
- Filter by keywords in names, descriptions, or other text fields
- Combine search with date filters for specific time periods

### 11.2 Filtering Options
- Filter medications by type or status
- Filter appointments by date range
- Filter documents by category

## 12. Mobile Access

### 12.2 Mobile Best Practices

## 13. Troubleshooting Common Issues

### 13.1 Login Problems
- **Forgot Password**: Use the "Forgot Password" link to reset your password
- **Account Locked**: Contact support if you believe your account is locked
- **Email Verification**: Check your spam folder for verification emails

### 13.2 Data Entry Issues
- **Required Fields**: Ensure all required fields are completed
- **Date Formats**: Use the date picker or enter dates in YYYY-MM-DD format
- **File Uploads**: Check file size and format restrictions

### 13.3 Sync Problems
- Refresh the page if data appears outdated
- Check your internet connection
- Clear browser cache if problems persist

## 14. Security and Privacy

### 14.1 Data Protection

### 14.2 Privacy Controls

### 14.3 Best Security Practices
- Use strong, unique passwords
- Log out of shared devices

## 17. Glossary

- **Care Recipient**: The person receiving care (sometimes referred to as patient)
- **Caregiver**: The person providing care and managing information
- **RLS**: Row Level Security - database feature ensuring data privacy
- **HIPAA**: Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act - US privacy law
- **PDF**: Portable Document Format - file format for document sharing
